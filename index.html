Here's what we'd do with it.

The sponsorship

Ant International is a global sponsor of the Laver Cup, and Carlos Alcaraz is Ant's global ambassador. Alipay+ owns the in-arena branding and the big consumer OOH across London.

Our slice

WorldFirst, as an Ant brand, gets a share: a spot on the shared fan-zone booth, a confirmed hospitality allocation (gala, breakfast, match sessions), and the Laver Cup logo. No player images on our channels; no Alcaraz activity in the UK outside the tournament.

What we propose

Leverage the Laver Cup for two WorldFirst wins: a brand lift in the UK, our weakest market, and the key accounts we most want to acquire. The play centres on where the crowd already is: a branded game at the O2, a pre-match bar, disciplined hospitality — with OOH costed for a keep-or-park call.

Red flag on Alcaraz

He is Ant's global ambassador, but he comes to play the Laver Cup and leaves. No appearances, no shoot, no UK activity we can build around him. Plan for zero Alcaraz access outside the arena, and treat every player image as Ant's to clear, not ours.

First question for the sponsorship team: does the tennis activation run WorldFirst-only or Ant-wide? The rest is laid out by date, 14 → 27 September.

Three ways to play it.

Option 1 · The game, at the crowd

Match week, at the O2 — the sports-review direction. The Logo Shot build (next pages), with two venue routes: space on the O2 estate, asked via the sponsorship team or priced as an agency build — or the padel club at the door: Padel Social Club sits inside Peninsula Square, two courts today, full clubhouse coming, corporate hire by enquiry. Option 2 · Book the bar

One reserved bar before one session — the pre-match page later in the deck. Zero build, drinks plus a reservation, and it doubles as the gifting moment. If we want grand instead of casual, Greenwich offers the Royal Naval College and Cutty Sark for a landmark drinks evening.

Option 3 · The private KA day

A classy tennis-club day on grass — knock-out afternoon, champagne reception, dinner: the Queen's Club formula. A true KA relationship play, and best kept outside the Laver window — it stands on its own clock rather than borrowing the Cup's.

Option 1 carries the brand-lift goal; option 2 is hospitality glue; option 3 is KA relationship-building. Different budgets, different owners, different clocks. The pages that follow detail option 1's game.

KA EVENT · THE GAMES
G
Why a game at all · then the two formats

Hit the logo. Win the point.

We hire a British tennis legend and turn them into a game — fun on the spot, real engagement, content we own. A certified umpire calls it, for the show of it.

The Logo Shot — everyone plays

An enclosed hitting alley, a giant WorldFirst wall at the end. Hit the wall, you're on the board. Ring the O, take home a printed ball. Through the O, the day's big prize. A kids' wall and an adults' wall; every go filmed and sent by QR.

One point v the legend — the show

You — or you and a teammate — against the pro, our logo on the net. One point, ~90 seconds, winnable enough that everyone tries. With a big-serve legend it becomes Return the Rocket: survive one serve, the crowd roars.
