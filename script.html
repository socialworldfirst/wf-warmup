Talk track · not for sharing

What to say when I walk the UK team through this.

Written the way it should sound out loud, not the way the deck reads. Skip anything that is already obvious in the room. The deck carries the detail, so I only need to carry the argument.

About 12 minutes if I keep moving, plus questions. The deck is 33 pages, but I only present about eight of them and let the rest sit there as backup.

Open · 45 seconds
Before I share the screen

"Ant sponsors the Laver Cup. It is at the O2 in September. We get a slice of that, and I want to walk you through two things: what we already hold, which is now confirmed, and what I think we should do with it, which is still open."

"The reason I want your input is that most of the decisions left are yours. Where we spend, who we invite, and whether we do anything beyond turning up."

Land this

This is not a request for approval. It is a request for direction on the open half.

Part 1 · 3 minutes
Deck: The tournament, six pages. Show the ticket table and the reading page.

"Start with what is real, because it is more than people think. We hold 131 tickets across the week. Forty one of those are hospitality, so free to us, and ninety we bought, which cost just over US$47,000."

"The free allocation is small and specific. Three seats at the opening gala, out of four hundred in the room. Six at the Federer breakfast, and that whole room is only forty people, so it is the scarcest thing we have all week. Then six hospitality tickets for every match session."

"One correction I want to flag, because it changed how I read this. Those numbers are WorldFirst totals, not per business line. So when we say three gala seats, that is three for all of us, not three each. That is why the gala list currently has five names against three seats."

Land this

We are richer in tickets than we think, and poorer in the special rooms than we think. The scarce thing is the breakfast, not the seats.

Expect
"Can we get more gala seats?"
Two extra are already requested. If they do not land, two names come off. Worth deciding now who those two are rather than in September.
"Why did we spend forty seven thousand on tickets?"
Because seat level drives the bill, not headcount. Prices run from US$186 to US$968 a seat. If that number needs to come down next time, we move people up a level, we do not cut guests.
Part 2 · 2 minutes
Deck: Hosting the week, four pages. Do not read them, just flip.

"The week itself mostly runs itself. Gala on the Thursday, play from Friday, the Federer breakfast on Saturday morning, the final on Sunday. Our job there is guest lists and looking after people, not creativity."

"The one thing I would push on is the breakfast. Six seats, C level only, and right now the list is almost entirely Enterprise. I think one of those should sit with Business, held for UK leadership."

Land this

This part is execution, not debate. The only real choice is who gets the six breakfast seats.

Part 3 · 90 seconds
Deck: Welcome drinks. Show the overview and the two waves page.

"On the Saturday we are hosting a client drinks reception at All Bar One, right on Peninsula Square. Sixty clients, three hours, then everyone walks into the evening session together."

"The bit I like is that it is not invented. The two waves in the plan, forty six people and sixteen people, are exactly our Saturday evening and Saturday afternoon ticket holders. So the reception and the ticket list are the same list. It is about three to five thousand pounds plus whatever we spend on the gift."

Land this

This one is already sensible and already booked. It needs a budget nod, not a redesign.

Part 4 · 5 minutes, the real conversation
Deck: the ideas. Fan zone, then the tennis event choice, then bar, then OOH.

Fan zone first, because it is free. "There is a shared Ant booth at the O2 entrance for about two weeks before the tournament, with a serve challenge on it. We do not have to build it, book it or permit it. For me that is the first thing to lock and the last thing to cut, and mostly what it buys us is two weeks to shoot the pictures we then use all year."

Then slow down. This next part is the decision.

The tennis event. "I want to do something of our own with tennis. There are two honest versions of that and they are not variations of each other, so I would rather we pick one than blend them."

"Option one is at the O2 during the tournament. There is a padel club that sits inside Peninsula Square, in the old mini golf spot, two courts today and a full clubhouse opening later this year. They take corporate bookings. So we could run a game where the crowd already is, on the days they are already there. That serves the brand goal, because it is public and it is tied to the event."

"Option two is a private day at a proper tennis club, something like Queen's. Knock out tournament in the afternoon, champagne and prize giving, then dinner. That is a relationship play for top accounts, and it is lovely, but it is a different thing."

"My own view, and I want to be argued with: option two can happen any time of year, so spending our Laver Cup window on it feels like a waste of the connection. The counter argument, which is fair, is that tennis is the connection, and a London tennis institution in Laver Cup week is a perfectly good warm up."

Land this

One is for the brand and it only works during the Cup. The other is for the accounts and it works any time. So which goal are we spending this money on.

Expect
"What does it cost?"
Around sixty to a hundred thousand at the O2 once the venue is in, or around forty to seventy for the private club day. Both are estimates, not quotes.
"Who runs it, us or Ant?"
That is the question I need the sponsorship team to answer. If it runs Ant wide, the build is shared and every business unit invites. If it is only us, we carry it. It changes the budget completely, so I do not want to brief anything until we know.
"Do we even need a hired player?"
A recognisable British name is what makes people say yes to the invitation and makes the footage worth cutting. But it is the single biggest line, so it is the first thing to drop if we want the cheap version.

Then the bar idea, briefly. "Someone will ask why we do not just book a nice bar. We effectively have, on the Saturday. Greenwich is mostly classic pubs, so there is no obvious upgrade nearby. There are landmark venues, the Old Royal Naval College and the Cutty Sark, but those are a budget step up, not a swap."

And OOH last, on purpose. "I have costed outdoor advertising so we can decide rather than wonder. The cheapest credible version at North Greenwich station is about six to nine thousand for a fortnight. A station takeover is thirty to sixty. The big digital drum at the entrance is twenty five to seventy five. My honest recommendation is that we park it, because the money buys more in the fan zone and the event, and take the free option, which is asking the station to write the event on their message boards."

Land this

OOH is not rejected on instinct. It is costed, and it loses to the alternatives at this budget.

Close · 1 minute
Stop sharing the screen for this bit.

"So the four things I need from you."

  • Guest lists, especially the gala. Five names, three seats. That needs settling quickly.
  • The tennis event: at the O2 during the Cup, or the private club day on its own clock. I lean to the O2.
  • Whether we are comfortable asking Ant to make the tennis activation an all business unit thing, because that changes what it costs us.
  • OOH: keep or park. I would park it.

Everything else in the deck is background. Send it round afterwards so people can read the ticket tables and the drinks plan in their own time.

If I only get three minutes

"We hold 131 tickets and about fifty thousand dollars of them are already bought, so the hosting is real and it is happening. The open question is whether we do anything of our own on top. I want to run a branded game at the O2 during the Cup, use the free fan zone booth for two weeks of content, and park outdoor advertising because I have priced it and it does not earn its place. What I need from you is the guest lists and a view on the game."
