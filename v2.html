Tournament week, 22–27 September · who holds what, then what "Rocky Club" means

The Laver Week.

What Ant holds that week
  • All in-arena branding — boards, walls, screens — seen in 234 broadcast markets
  • The big consumer OOH across London, and Alcaraz
  • The larger share of VIP hospitality, and all official photography
What WorldFirst holds that week
  • A place on the shared fan-zone booth, and the Laver Cup logo
  • A confirmed allocation: 3 gala · 6 breakfast · 6 per match session — WorldFirst seats, split across our three business lines
  • Our own channels, our own people, our own banners — and whatever red we choose to add on the street

What "Rocky Club" means. It's the top hospitality tier — all five sessions plus the VIP experiences (back-of-house tour, player photos, courtside practice). Following the sponsorship review, WorldFirst's allocation is confirmed (the ticket table is two pages on). What's still open: which ticket tier carries those VIP experiences.

Red flag on Alcaraz: he plays the Laver Cup and leaves — no appearances, no shoot, no UK activity we can build around him, and every player image is Ant's to clear, not ours. No player images on our channels; no WF photographer inside; official shots arrive via a shared folder each day. Our budget works the streets, the fan zone and our own channels — not the arena.

THE TENNIS EVENT · THE CHOICEIdea
2
One idea, two very different shapes

A tennis event.

We host a tennis moment of our own around the Cup. There are two honest versions of that, and they are not variations of each other — different venues, different guests, different weeks, different reasons. They should be chosen between, not blended.

Option 1 · Around the O2, Laver week

Brand lift, at the crowd

A game built where the tournament crowd already is — the padel club at the O2's door, or space on the estate. Public, visible, content-first. Serves the brand goal.

Option 2 · The private KA day

Relationships, on our own clock

A classic tennis-club day for top accounts at an iconic London venue. Private, high-touch, invitation-only. Serves the account goal — and works outside the Laver window.

The pages that follow set out each option, then the parts they share: who is in the room, the games, the host, the take-home and the cost.

Padel Social Club sits inside Peninsula Square, SE10 0DX — the same square as the O2 entrance, in the old mini-golf and wine-bar spot. Two outdoor courts run there today, with a full club, clubhouse, bar and shop due to open later this year. They take corporate bookings by enquiry.

Or the estate itself

The second route is space on the O2 estate during tournament week, asked through the sponsorship team or priced as a temporary build. Higher ceiling, slower answer, and the one that needs Ant's help.

Why this one serves the brief

It is tied to the event and to the crowd, which is exactly the brand-lift goal. Nothing to construct, a venue a minute from the arena doors, and a UK team visit already offered to see it in person.

Option 2 · the private KA day, on its own clock

A day at a real tennis club.

The venue and the formula

Queen's Club — described by our UK team as a smaller Wimbledon, and one of the most iconic tennis venues in London. Their standard corporate day: a knock-out tournament through the afternoon, then a champagne reception and prize giving, then dinner in a private dining room.

queensclub.co.uk · sporting days ↗

The open argument — worth settling

One view: outside the tennis itself it is not really connected to the Laver Cup, so it should sit in its own window. The other: tennis is the connection — a London tennis tournament, a London tennis institution, a classy warm-up in the way the grass season warms up Wimbledon.

The practical catch either way: if it runs close to the Cup, be careful who is invited — hosting people who are not coming to the Laver Cup creates an awkward second guest list.

KA EVENT · THE GAMES
G
Why a game at all · then the two formats

Hit the logo. Win the point.

We hire a British tennis legend and turn them into a game — fun on the spot, real engagement, content we own. A certified umpire calls it, for the show of it.

The Logo Shot — everyone plays

An enclosed hitting alley, a giant WorldFirst wall at the end. Hit the wall, you're on the board. Ring the O, take home a printed ball. Through the O, the day's big prize. A kids' wall and an adults' wall; every go filmed and sent by QR.

One point v the legend — the show

You — or you and a teammate — against the pro, our logo on the net. One point, ~90 seconds, winnable enough that everyone tries. With a big-serve legend it becomes Return the Rocket: survive one serve, the crowd roars.

BOOK THE BARIdea
BAR
A separate idea · drinks, not tennis

Book the bar.

We have already done the sensible version

The client welcome drinks on Saturday 26 is exactly this idea, booked: All Bar One on Peninsula Square, three hours, then straight into the arena. That is the baseline, and it is covered earlier in this deck.

The local reality

Greenwich is short on smart bars and clubs — it is mostly classic pubs. So "find somewhere nicer nearby" is harder than it sounds, and the O2 estate itself stays the practical choice for anything tied to a match session.

If we want grand instead

Greenwich has landmark venues for hire — parts of the Old Royal Naval College, the Queen's House, the Cutty Sark. Very impressive, and very expensive: a step up in budget, not a swap.

Two concepts, now costed — for a keep-or-park call

OOH at the O2.

Concept A · borrow the approved asset

Alipay+'s banner runs Carlos Alcaraz, and that image is already approved by his team. So the easy leverage is a WorldFirst version of the same asset: our logo, our message, on artwork that is cleared. Borrowed fame, least friction.

Concept B · Team Europe v Team World

Split one board down the middle, half blue and half red, with WorldFirst on the world side. No player image, so nothing to clear beyond the Laver Cup lockup we hold. Less borrowed, more brand — a smart play on their own format.

Both are directions, not decisions — and we're open to a third. The Cup is on our home turf; if there's a better way to use the streets, bring it.
